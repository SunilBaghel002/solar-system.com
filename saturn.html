Saturn has the second-shortest day in the solar system. One day on Saturn takes only 10.7 hours (the time it takes for Saturn to rotate or spin around once), and Saturn makes a complete orbit around the Sun (a year in Saturnian time) in about 29.4 Earth years (10,756 Earth days).

SURFACE

Surface. As a gas giant, Saturn doesn't have a true surface. The planet is mostly swirling gases and liquids deeper down. While a spacecraft would have nowhere to land on Saturn, it wouldn't be able to fly through unscathed either.

AXIAL TILT

Saturn's axial tilt is 26.73 degrees, which is similar to Earth's 23.5-degree tilt. This tilt is caused by the gradual motion of Saturn's moons, which triggered a resonance phenomenon that continues to this day. The interaction between Saturn's axis and Neptune's path gradually tilted Saturn's axis until it reached its current inclination.

ATMOSPHERE

Saturn is approximately 75% hydrogen and 25% helium with traces of other substances like methane and water ice. Saturn's atmosphere, although similar to Jupiter's, is much less interesting to look at from a distance.

MAGNETIC FIELD

The magnetic field strength at Saturn's equator is about 21 μT (0.21 G), which corresponds to a dipole magnetic moment of about 4.6 × 1018 T•m 3. This makes Saturn's magnetic field slightly weaker than Earth's; however, its magnetic moment is about 580 times larger.
